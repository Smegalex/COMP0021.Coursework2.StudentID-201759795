Common name: Red Panda

Scientific name: Ailurus fulgens

The Red Panda is a small arboreal mammal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China.

About Red Pandas

Red Pandas are known for their distinctive red fur and bushy tail, which they use for balance while climbing trees.

They are primarily herbivores, feeding mainly on bamboo, but they may also consume fruits, berries, and insects.

Conservation Status

The Red Panda is classified as Endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).

Threats to their survival include habitat loss, poaching, and fragmentation of their forest habitat.
