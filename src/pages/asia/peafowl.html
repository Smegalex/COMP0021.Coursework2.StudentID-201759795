Common name: Indian Peafowl

Scientific name: Pavo cristatus

The Indian Peafowl, also known as the Peacock, is a large and colorful bird native to the Indian subcontinent.

About Indian Peafowls

Indian Peafowls are known for their striking plumage, especially the male, which displays vibrant blue, green, and gold feathers during courtship.

They are omnivorous birds, feeding on a variety of seeds, insects, fruits, and small animals.

Conservation Status

The Indian Peafowl is listed as Least Concern by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).

Although they face threats such as habitat loss and hunting, their large and widespread population helps ensure their survival.
